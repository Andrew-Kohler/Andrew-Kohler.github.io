About the Project

The Echoic Reverie is a 2D platformer about seeing through sound: interpreting the world audiably rather than visually, and listening to the 'chorus of the mundane.' This strange dreamscape of a carnival was made to provide a unique and understandable user experience to the player for our final project in UX Design. I was largely responsible for the game's design as well as programming its sound-responsive environment and soundscape.

This is one of my personal favorites - it's so aesthetically wonderful, fun to interact with, and weird. It tells a complete, if small, story in the 5 minutes of game there are to play.

Programming Accomplishments

  • Created and programmatically integrated particle systems that visually and functionally act as soundwaves to reveal parts of the environment
  • Programmed the interactive audio elements, platforms, and props within the environment

Other Accomplishments

  • Created all sprites for the game, including player, enemies, and environment
  • Lead the design process and prototyping that formed the foundation of the game's features and aesthetic
